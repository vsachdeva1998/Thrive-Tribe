Business Associate Agreement

This Business Associate Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of [Effective Date] (the "Effective Date"), by and between [CLIENT] ("CLIENT") and [CEC] ("CEC"), collectively referred to as the "Parties" and individually as a "Party," pursuant to a Master Services Agreement ("MSA") dated [Date].

WHEREAS

CLIENT is a covered entity as defined under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA), Public Law 104-191, and has engaged CEC to perform services under the MSA, which may involve the disclosure and use of protected health information ("PHI") as defined herein;

The Parties intend to safeguard the privacy of PHI disclosed to CEC in compliance with HIPAA, the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act ("HITECH Act"), and other applicable laws;

NOW, THEREFORE

In consideration of the mutual promises and covenants contained herein and in the MSA, the Parties agree as follows:

I. Definitions

  • (a) Breach: "Breach" means the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI in a manner not permitted under the Privacy Rule, which compromises the security or privacy of PHI.
  • (b) Designated Record Set: "Designated Record Set" refers to a group of records maintained by or for a covered entity that includes: Medical and billing records about individuals maintained by or for a health care provider; Enrollment, payment, claims adjudication, and case or medical management records maintained by or for a health plan; Records used to make decisions about individuals.
  • (c) Electronic Health Record: "Electronic Health Record" refers to an electronic record of health-related information created, gathered, managed, and consulted by authorized healthcare clinicians and staff.
  • (d) Electronic Protected Health Information (EPHI): "EPHI" shall have the meaning set forth in 45 C.F.R. §160.103.
  • (e) Individual: "Individual" shall have the meaning set forth in 45 C.F.R. 160.103, including a person qualifying as a personal representative under 45 C.F.R. 164.502(g).
  • (f) Individually Identifiable Health Information: Information that: Is created or received by a health care provider, health plan, employer, or health care clearinghouse; Relates to the health or condition of an individual, provision of health care, or payment for health care; and identifies the individual or has a reasonable basis to believe it can be used to identify the individual.
  • (g) Limited Data Set: PHI excluding direct identifiers such as names, addresses, phone numbers, and Social Security numbers, among others.
  • (h) Privacy Rule: Standards for Privacy of Individually Identifiable Health Information at 45 C.F.R. parts 160 and 164, subparts A and E.
  • (i) Protected Health Information (PHI): PHI as defined in 45 C.F.R. 160.103, limited to information created or received by CEC from or on behalf of CLIENT.
  • (j) Security Rule: Standards for the Protection of Electronic Protected Health Information at 45 C.F.R. parts 160 and 164, Subparts A and C.
  • (k) Required By Law: "Required By Law" as defined in 45 C.F.R. 164.103.
  • (l) Secretary: The Secretary of the Department of Health and Human Services or their designee.
  • (m) Unsecured Protected Health Information (Unsecured PHI): PHI that is not rendered unusable, unreadable, or indecipherable to unauthorized individuals using technology or methodologies specified by the Secretary.

II. Termination of Previous Business Associate Agreement

Any prior Business Associate Agreement between CLIENT and CEC is terminated and superseded by this Agreement.

III. Obligations and Activities of CEC

  • (a) CEC shall use or disclose PHI only as permitted by this Agreement and 42 C.F.R. §164.504(e). Violations may result in civil or criminal penalties as per Sections 1176 and 1177 of the Social Security Act.
  • (b) CEC shall not use or disclose PHI except as permitted by this Agreement or required by law.
  • (c) CEC shall implement appropriate safeguards to prevent unauthorized use or disclosure of PHI.
  • (d) CEC shall mitigate any known harmful effects of unauthorized PHI use or disclosure.
  • (e) CEC shall report to CLIENT any unauthorized PHI use or disclosure.
  • (f) CEC shall ensure that any agent or subcontractor receiving PHI agrees to the same restrictions and conditions as CEC under this Agreement.
  • (g) CEC shall provide CLIENT access to any PHI in a Designated Record Set held by CEC, or to an Individual as directed by CLIENT.
  • (h) CEC shall amend any PHI in a Designated Record Set as directed by CLIENT or an Individual.
  • (i) CEC shall make available its internal practices, books, and records relating to PHI to CLIENT or the Secretary for compliance purposes.
  • (j) CEC shall document PHI disclosures to enable CLIENT to respond to accounting requests as required by 45 C.F.R. 164.528.
  • (k) Accounting of Disclosures: CEC shall provide CLIENT or an Individual with information regarding PHI disclosures to enable compliance with 45 C.F.R. 164.528. CEC shall keep an accounting of all disclosures of Electronic Health Records for three years prior to the request date. CEC shall provide the accounting of disclosures to CLIENT or the Individual as prescribed by the Privacy Rule.
  • (l) Security Rule – Related Provisions for EPHI: CEC shall implement Administrative, Physical, and Technical Safeguards to protect EPHI. CEC shall implement policies and procedures required by the Security Rule. Violations may result in civil or criminal penalties. CEC shall ensure that agents or subcontractors implementing safeguards for EPHI. CEC shall report Security Incidents to CLIENT.
  • (m) Breach Notification: CEC shall notify CLIENT of any breach of Unsecured PHI, providing details of the breach and mitigation efforts. Notification must occur within ten (10) business days of discovery. CEC shall assist CLIENT with breach investigations.
  • (n) Requested Restrictions on Certain Disclosures: CEC shall comply with restrictions requested by an Individual unless otherwise required by law.
  • (o) Minimum Necessary: CEC shall limit its use, disclosure, or request of PHI to the minimum necessary or to a Limited Data Set.
  • (p) Prohibition on Sale of PHI: CEC shall not receive remuneration for PHI without CLIENT’s approval and a valid authorization from the individual.
  • (q) Access to Information in Electronic Format: CEC shall provide electronic copies of PHI upon request, without charging more than labor costs.
  • (r) Marketing: CEC shall not perform marketing using PHI without CLIENT’s approval.
  • (s) Audits: CEC acknowledges it is subject to audits by the Secretary to ensure compliance with applicable laws.

IV. Permitted Uses and Disclosures by CEC

  • (a) CEC may use or disclose PHI on behalf of CLIENT in accordance with the MSA and applicable laws.
  • (b) CEC may use PHI for its own management and administrative purposes.
  • (c) CEC may disclose PHI for proper management and administration, provided reasonable assurances of confidentiality are obtained.
  • (d) CEC may use PHI for Data Aggregation services as permitted by 45 C.F.R. 164.504(e)(2)(i)(B).
  • (e) CEC may use PHI to report violations of law.

V. Obligations of CLIENT

  • (a) CLIENT shall provide CEC with its notice of privacy practices and any changes to it.
  • (b) CLIENT shall notify CEC of any changes in permission or restrictions on PHI.
  • (c) CLIENT shall not request PHI disclosures in a manner not permissible under the Privacy Rule.

VI. Term and Termination

  • (a) Term: This Agreement is effective as of the date first written above and terminates when all PHI is returned or destroyed.
  • (b) Termination for Cause: The non-breaching Party shall provide an opportunity to cure a material breach or terminate the Agreement if not cured. Immediate termination may occur if CEC is involved in a criminal HIPAA violation. If neither termination nor cure is feasible, the non-breaching Party shall report to the Secretary.
  • (c) Immediate Termination: CLIENT may terminate the Agreement immediately if CEC faces a criminal proceeding for HIPAA violations.
  • (d) Effect of Termination: CEC shall return or destroy all PHI or, if infeasible, extend the protections of this Agreement to the retained PHI.

VII. Miscellaneous

  • (a) Regulatory References: References to sections in the Privacy Rule include any amendments or successor sections.
  • (b) Amendment: The Parties agree to amend this Agreement as necessary to comply with applicable laws.
  • (c) Survival: The provisions of Section VI(d) shall survive termination of this Agreement.
  • (d) Interpretation: This Agreement shall prevail over any conflicting provisions in related services agreements, with ambiguities resolved in favor of compliance with applicable laws.
