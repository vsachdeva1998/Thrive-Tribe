Master Services Agreement

Date: February 27, 2024

This Master Services Agreement (the “Agreement” or “MSA”), dated as of February 27, 2024 (the “Effective Date”), is entered into by and between:

Client: [Insert Client’s Name], a [Dental Office/DSO/Billing Office] with its principal place of business at [Insert Address] (hereinafter referred to as “Client”),

CEC: [Insert CEC’s Name], a company with its principal place of business at [Insert Address] (hereinafter referred to as “CEC”),

Collectively referred to as the “Parties” and individually as a “Party.”

WHEREAS

Client is a dental group practice or dental practice management company;

CEC, along with its parent company Clinical Excel Computech LLP based in India, has the capacity to provide certain services, including but not limited to eligibility/benefits verification, payment posting, claims tracing/AR follow-ups, and special time projects (collectively, the “Services”);

Client desires to engage CEC to provide such Services on its behalf;

The Parties wish to set forth the terms and conditions under which Client will purchase from CEC, and CEC will provide to Client, such Services as may be requested by Client from time to time;

NOW, THEREFORE

In consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements herein contained, the Parties agree as follows:

1. Scope of Services

CEC agrees to provide, and Client agrees to purchase, Services on a task-by-task and, where applicable, customer-by-customer basis in accordance with this Agreement. The specific Services and work product to be provided by CEC will be detailed in individual Exhibits of Work (each an “Exhibit”).

Each Exhibit will specify the Services to be performed, compensation terms, and any additional terms and conditions. Exhibits shall be incorporated into this Agreement upon execution by both Parties. CEC is not obligated to provide Services, and Client is not obligated to retain or compensate CEC for any Services, unless a mutually signed Exhibit is in place.

2. Term

This Agreement will commence on the Effective Date and will continue until terminated by either Party in accordance with this Agreement. Any Exhibits of Work will be deemed automatically terminated upon termination of this Agreement.

3. Exhibits

  • a. A reference to this Agreement, incorporating its terms.
  • b. The effective date and term of the Services to be provided.
  • c. A description of the Services.
  • d. Fees, charges, and a payment schedule (“Schedule of Fees”).
  • e. Any additional provisions applicable to the Services.

Exhibits become effective only when signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

4. Payment for Services

Subject to CEC meeting agreed performance metrics, Client will pay CEC for Services as detailed in the Exhibit. If Client defaults on payment, CEC may terminate the Exhibit if the default is not cured within ten (10) days of written notice. Unpaid amounts will accrue interest at the lesser of (i) 1.5% per month or (ii) the maximum rate allowed by law.

5. Performance of Services and Warranties

CEC will perform Services diligently, in good faith, and professionally, adhering to any agreed performance metrics. CEC will not engage in reckless or willful misconduct.

6. Confidentiality

  • a. Each Party will maintain the confidentiality of the terms of this Agreement, Exhibits, and non-public information received (“Confidential Information”), except as necessary for performance, legal requirements, or to legal and accounting professionals.
  • b. Each Party will protect the Confidential Information with precautions at least as stringent as those used for its own confidential information.
  • c. Confidential Information does not include information that is public knowledge, rightfully in possession of the receiving Party, developed independently, required by law, or disclosed in response to a valid court order.
  • d. Confidentiality obligations survive the termination of this Agreement.
  • e. The receiving Party may use Confidential Information in accordance with written authorization from the disclosing Party.

7. Indemnification

  • a. CEC will indemnify Client from third-party claims arising from CEC’s negligence or misconduct, including violations of HIPAA regulations.
  • b. Client will indemnify CEC from third-party claims resulting from Client’s instructions, infringement claims related to Client-provided materials, modifications not authorized by CEC, combinations with non-CEC materials, or breaches of agreements with third parties.

8. Termination

Either Party may terminate the Agreement and all Exhibits without cause with thirty (30) days' written notice. Upon termination, both Parties will return or destroy Confidential Information and certify such action within thirty (30) days.

9. Binding Nature and Assignment

This Agreement binds the Parties and their successors and assigns. Assignment to a third party requires written consent.

10. Insurance

Client will include CEC in its insurance coverage with minimum limits of Five hundred thousand dollars for Errors & Omissions, General Liability, and Cyber Security.

11. Force Majeure

Neither Party is liable for failure to perform due to acts of God, natural disasters, war, or other uncontrollable events.

12. Notices

Notices must be in writing and delivered by facsimile, electronic mail, or certified mail to the addresses provided.

13. Severability

If any provision is invalid or unenforceable, it will be severed, and the remaining provisions will be enforced.

14. Waiver

Waivers or amendments must be in writing and signed by the Party against whom enforcement is sought.

15. Relationship of Parties

CEC acts as an independent contractor. This Agreement does not create an employer-employee, principal-agent, joint venture, or partnership relationship.

16. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties, superseding any prior agreements or understandings.

17. Ownership

  • a. Client retains ownership of its pre-existing systems, software, documentation, and intellectual property.
  • b. CEC retains ownership of its pre-existing systems, software, documentation, and intellectual property.

18. Non-Solicitation

Neither Party will solicit or hire the other’s employees during the Agreement term and for two years thereafter, without written consent.

19. Other Matters

Client is responsible for applicable sales or use taxes related to this Agreement, excluding CEC’s income taxes.

20. Choice of Law; Venue

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of New Jersey. Disputes will be resolved in New Jersey state or federal courts.
